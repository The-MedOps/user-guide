Frequently Asked Questions

Getting in

I didn’t get my invitation email

Check your spam folder first. If it’s still missing, ask your Tenant Admin to
re-send your invitation — they can generate a fresh setup link for you at any
time before you’ve set a password.

I forgot my password

Use Forgot password on the sign-in page. MedOps emails you a one-time
code and a reset link. After resetting, you’ll need to sign in again on each
device. See Signing In & The Basics.

Why was I signed out?

MedOps signs you out automatically after a period of inactivity to protect
patient information. The timer resets while you’re working. Your Tenant Admin
sets how long the system waits (15 minutes by default).

Can two people use the same front-desk computer?

Yes. MedOps keeps each role’s sign-in separate, so different roles can be
signed in on a shared computer without clashing.

Patients & appointments

How does a patient get their MRN?

It’s assigned automatically the moment you register the patient — you never
create one yourself, and it never changes.

Why can’t I book a doctor at a certain time?

The time is likely outside the doctor’s working hours, during their leave or a
blocked time, or already booked. MedOps shows a doctor’s availability and
hides times that can’t be used, so only valid slots can be chosen.

Can I change which hospital a patient belongs to?

No. A patient’s registered hospital is fixed so their records stay anchored to
where they were first seen. Everything else about the patient can be edited.

Care & billing

Do I need to create the bill after a consultation?

No. Completing a consultation automatically issues the prescription, creates
the consultation bill, and marks the appointment completed. The front desk
just records the payment.

How does the pharmacy get the prescription?

Through the prescription’s reference number. The pharmacist enters it to pull
up the medicines, then dispenses and bills them at the pharmacy’s prices. See
Pharmacy Portal.

A medicine I need isn’t in the list

Doctors can add a new medicine to the catalogue while writing a prescription,
so it never holds up the visit. Tenant Admins can also add, edit and remove
medicines. See Medicines Catalogue.

What happens to a bed after a patient leaves?

It’s flagged reserved for cleaning automatically after a discharge or
transfer, so the front desk only assigns beds that are ready.

Roles & access

Why can’t I see a feature I expected?

MedOps shows only what your role needs. If you think you should have access to
something, ask your Tenant Admin to check your account. See
Roles at a Glance.

Who can see the activity log?

The activity log is available to the Tenant Admin, giving a single view of
who did what and when across the organisation. See
Activity Log.

Can I edit my leave after it starts?

No — leave and time-off can be changed or removed only before they begin, so
add upcoming absences ahead of time. See
My Appointments & Availability.
