An Inpatient Admission

This guide follows an inpatient (IP) stay from admission to discharge, and
who does what along the way.

Who’s involved

Operations (front desk), the Doctor, and Nurses. Make sure your
ward layout — floors, rooms and beds — is set up first.
Set up beds →

The journey

  1. Find or register the patient (Operations)
    Search for the patient, or register them if they’re new.
    Patients →
  2. Admit to a bed (Operations)
    Choose a vacant bed and the admitting doctor, record the reason for
    admission, and capture any advance deposit, payment type and insurance
    details. The bed is marked occupied.
    Admitted Patients →
  3. Round on the patient (Doctor)
    Record each ward round with notes, vitals, your assessment and the plan.
    The whole care team can see the history.
  4. Manage medications (Doctor)
    Add and adjust inpatient medicines as treatment changes during the stay.
  5. Transfer if needed (Operations)
    If the patient needs a different bed (for example, the ICU), transfer them
    to another vacant bed. The same admission continues with all its history.
  6. Discharge (Operations)
    When the patient is ready to leave, process the discharge. MedOps records
    the discharge date and frees the bed for cleaning.
  7. Settle charges (Operations)
    The room’s daily tariff and the advance deposit inform the final
    settlement; any dispensed medicines are billed by the pharmacy.
    Billing & Payments →
Beds look after themselves

After a discharge or transfer, the bed is automatically flagged for cleaning,
so the front desk only ever assigns beds that are truly ready.
