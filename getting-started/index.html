Signing In & The Basics

This page covers everything you need to get into MedOps and find your way
around for the first time.

How you get an account

There are two ways an account is created:

  • Your administrator invites you. Most people (doctors, nurses, staff,
    operations and pharmacy users) are added by their organisation’s Tenant
    Admin and receive an email invitation to set up their password.
  • You sign up to start a new organisation. If you’re setting up MedOps
    for your hospital group for the first time, you sign up as the Tenant
    Admin    , which also creates your organisation.
Most users don’t sign up

If your hospital already uses MedOps, you don’t sign up — you wait for an
invitation email and set your password from the link inside it.

Setting up your password for the first time

When your administrator adds you, MedOps emails you a secure setup link (or
a one-time code, depending on your role).

  1. Open the invitation email
    Look for the email from MedOps with your login details and a setup link.
  2. Open the setup link
    The link takes you to a page where you choose your own password. Setup
    links are time-limited for security, so use yours soon after it arrives.
  3. Choose a strong password
    Your password must be at least 8 characters and include at least one
    uppercase letter, one number, and one special character
    (such as ! @ # $).
  4. Sign in
    Once your password is set, you’re signed in and ready to start.
Got a temporary password instead?

Some invitations include a temporary password. Sign in with it, and MedOps
will immediately ask you to set a new password of your own before you
continue.

Signing in

  1. Go to the sign-in page and enter your email address.
  2. Enter your password. If you haven’t set one yet, MedOps will email
    you a one-time code so you can set it up.
  3. You’re in. You’ll land on the home screen for your role.
Shared computers are fine

MedOps keeps each role’s sign-in separate. On a shared front-desk
computer, this means different roles can be signed in side by side without
interfering with one another.

Forgot your password?

  1. Choose “Forgot password” on the sign-in page and enter your email.
  2. Check your email. MedOps sends a one-time code and a reset link.
  3. Set a new password using the code or the link. The same password
    rules apply.
  4. Sign in again. For your security, resetting your password signs you
    out everywhere, so sign in again on each device.
Privacy first

For security, the reset screen always says the email was sent — it won’t
reveal whether an address is registered. If you don’t receive anything,
check the address and your spam folder, or ask your administrator.

Sessions and staying signed in

MedOps signs you out automatically after a period of inactivity, which
protects patient information on unattended screens.

  • The default is 15 minutes of inactivity.
  • Your organisation’s Tenant Admin can change this (anywhere from 1 minute
    up to 24 hours).
  • The timer resets while you’re working — you only get signed out if
    you step away and stop using the system.

Signing out

Use Sign out whenever you finish, especially on shared computers.
Signing out ends your session immediately.
