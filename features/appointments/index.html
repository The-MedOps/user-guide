Appointments

Appointments connect a patient with a doctor at a specific time. MedOps
checks each booking against the doctor’s real availability, so clashes are
prevented before they happen.

Who manages this

Operations staff and the Tenant Admin book and manage appointments.
Doctors see their own appointments in their queue but don’t book them.

Booking an appointment

When you book, you set:

  • Patient — the registered patient (this can’t be changed afterwards)
  • Doctor — optional, and must work at the patient’s hospital
  • Visit type — Outpatient (OP), Inpatient (IP), Emergency, Consultation,
    or Follow-up
  • Date and time — must be in the future
  • Reason for visit — optional
  • Bed — required for an inpatient (IP) visit; must be a vacant bed

Booking on someone’s behalf

For an outpatient visit booked for a family member or dependant, you can add
beneficiary details: their name and age (required together), plus an
optional weight and relationship to the registered patient.

Appointment statuses

An appointment moves through these states:

Status Meaning
Scheduled Booked and awaiting the visit (the usual starting point)
Admitted The starting point for an inpatient appointment
Completed The visit (and any consultation) is finished
Cancelled The appointment was cancelled
No-show The patient didn’t arrive

How MedOps prevents clashes

You can’t create an invalid booking
  • No past bookings — appointments must be in the future.
  • Within working hours — a doctor can only be booked during the hours
    they’ve set.
  • Not during leave or time-off — the doctor’s leave and blocked times
    are off-limits.
  • No double-booking — slots already taken for that doctor on that day
    are shown so they can’t be picked again.
  • Only free beds — an inpatient visit can only use a vacant bed.

When you choose a doctor and date, MedOps shows their upcoming leave and
blocked times, and which slots are already booked — so you can pick a free
time with confidence.

Rescheduling and updating

After booking, you can change:

  • The date and time (re-checked against the doctor’s availability)
  • The doctor (re-checked against the hospital and availability)
  • The visit type, reason, and beneficiary details
  • The status — for example, marking it completed, cancelled or
    no-show

The patient on an appointment can’t be changed — book a new appointment if
it’s for someone else.

From appointment to consultation

When a doctor opens one of their appointments and starts a consultation,
the visit record and the clinical record are linked. Completing the
consultation also completes the appointment and generates the relevant bills.
